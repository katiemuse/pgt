Build You App's Data Model


To build an app in the Salesforce Platform, you start with a data model. ‘Objects’ are the building blocks of that data model.



We'll start by creating a Customer Insights object to bring important customer information right into your app.

Did You Know?

When you create a custom object, you can use it throughout your Salesforce instance, not just the app or database you created it for.


Integrate Customer Ideas


Egg's customers use a mobile app built on Heroku to control their thermostats, report issues, and even share ideas on how to improve the product.

We can map data between Heroku and Salesforce.

Did You Know?

Heroku Connect is a two-way integration of data between Salesforce Lightning and Heroku


Using bi-directional synchronization, Heroku Connect unifies the data in your Heroku mobile app with your Salesforce database—contacts, accounts and other custom objects, such as the Customer Insights object we just created.

Now, the data from the app will map directly into Salesforce.


Predict Customers’ Net Promoter Score


We can use Einstein Builder to create a field that predicts how specific type of customer cases impacts customer’s Net Promoter Score. Einstein makes the prediction based on a few things you choose, like number device crashes, installation issues, and escalations.




Integrate Customer Orders From SAP


Salesforce Connect lets you bring in other external data. For this app, we'll pull in Sales Orders from Egg's SAP data base to let Product Managers know what and when customers purchased.

Ready for some coding? Just kidding. You can integrate that data into Salesforce with simple clicks.

Did You Know?

You can use the “Approval Process” tool to create approval processes for particular objects/records. An approval process specifies each step of approval, including from whom to request approval and what to do at each point of the process.
